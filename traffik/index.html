The Ride Against Traffik

In February 2019, 4 of us travelled from London to Delhi to join the Viva team and begin the challenge of riding one of the major traffiking routes, against the flow of young girls being bought and sold between Kathmandu and Delhi. The aim of the challenge, to raise awareness and funds to help Viva in preventing the traffik and restoring the lives of those involved.

Day 0

24th Feb London to Delhi

We arrived in Delhi around 9am, Gary met us at the airport with Nirendra, the driver and the 4x4 that was to be our transport for the fortnight. We wheeled the luggage out to the car park, located the vehicle and started to workout how to get everything in. Magically 3 or 4 men appeared, who seemed to profess some level of expertise in loading vehicles, offering their assistance and within 10 minutes it was established that the bikes would fit in with Gary and Nirendra and the four of us would get a taxi to the hotel. The taxi was a squeeze, but it was only a 40 minute drive and a good introduction to the roads of Delhi

I don't think more than 30 seconds passed without hearing a car horn the whole time we were in Delhi. We arrived at the hotel a bit tired and, as only one room was available initially, set about reconstructing the bikes. There had only been one minor issue in transit, Dave's bike had arrived with a broken QR skewer, thankfully we had a spare so after a couple of hours of assembling and checking we were good to go. Our rooms were ready just in time for a quick shower, a bite to eat and then we got to meet some of the Viva team in India.

Viva India had been spending the last few days in the hotel getting together the leaders from the various centres around India to discuss strategy and encourage each other. They spent about an hour telling us about each of the regions they worked in and the many ways in which they were helping children in those areas, it was good to hear the great work they are doing. After the session there was time for a quick but necessary snooze before heading out for tea with some of the Viva team. We headed to a restaurant round the corner from the hotel, where they proceeded to repeatedly bring delicious food and place it on our plates for at least 20 minutes before telling us that was just the starter and the main course was a buffet so we could help ourselves. Needless to say I think I stocked up well for the next day. When we finally made it back to the hotel and into bed I think I was asleep in seconds.

Day 1

25th Feb Delhi to Mathura

The alarms went off soon after 5am, showers were had, sun cream was applied, cycling kit was donned and bags were packed. Rooms were emptied, breakfast was eaten, the vehicle was packed and bikes were readied, all before 6am (mostly). The plan was to be out of Delhi before the traffic got bad, so shortly before 7, after a prayer and a goodbye from the members of the Viva team that were awake, Gary led the way out of the hotel parking area onto the road.

We waited for the 4x4 to catch up before winding our way on to the main road that would take us out of Delhi, the sun was just starting to rise and the city was starting to come to life. Nervousness was the pervasive feeling, unknown roads, unknown rules and an unknown destination with the added thought of only having put the bikes together a few hours earlier on very little sleep made the intial miles somewhat tentative.
